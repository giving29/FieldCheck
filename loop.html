The intended system · shown in prototype

An engine that gets truer the longer it runs.

FieldCheck isn't a static formula that scores you once. It's a loop: every read is a falsifiable bet, reality is the only label, and the gap between them teaches the engine to read truer next time. The longer it runs, the harder it is to beat — because it's trained on what actually happened, not on opinion.

This is the system we're building toward. The front of the loop runs today; the back half is the promise — stated out loud, on the record, so reality can hold us to it. We'll build it out over time, in the open.

● what learns today

Corroboration & correction

Cross-source agreement already moves confidence. A coach or athlete who disputes a read already teaches the engine. Every clip uploaded already sharpens the next read. The loop's front half is live.

○ what learns tomorrow

Trained on outcomes

As the dataset of (read → outcome) pairs grows, the engine will train directly on the only ground truth that matters — what actually happened. An honest number that measurably predicts. That's the promise we're building toward.

why this is uncopyable

A paid ranking never makes a falsifiable prediction — so it has nothing to be graded against.

The loop only runs if you're willing to be wrong on the record. FieldCheck is. That's why a competitor can copy a page but not the loop: they'd need to have started logging predictions years ago, and a pay-to-play model can't — its number is for sale, not for testing. Every day the loop runs, the moat deepens.

Said plainly: this page describes the system we intend, shown in prototype. Stages 1–3 are live on prod today. Stage 4's mechanism is live and accruing real outcomes over time. Stages 5–6 — the reinforcement signal and the self-tuning engine — are the promise, on our roadmap, not yet shipped. We're stating the vision out loud and building it in the open, because a number you can trust is one whose maker will be graded by reality.
