About FieldCheck

Built for the
kid who doesn't
get seen.

College recruiting is a market. Markets have information asymmetry. The athletes with the best access to information — coaches, trainers, club networks, private college counselors — win that market. FieldCheck is the other side of that trade.

Why this exists.

Every year, hundreds of Division I athletes are misread. Undersized players with elite Mindset scores don't get offers because a scout saw one bad practice. First-generation athletes from rural states don't get callbacks because no one in their family knows how to navigate the process. Transfer portal prospects get dismissed because their production numbers look thin on a stat sheet that doesn't account for what system they were running in.

FieldCheck runs the public record through a five-layer model that doesn't care about your club pedigree or your AAU circuit. It cares about what the evidence says. The polygon doesn't lie. The shape is either there or it isn't.

Six things FieldCheck
will never do.

The recruiting industry runs on conflicts of interest. FieldCheck is designed around what we refuse to build.

  • Charge athletes to see their own verdict
  • Sell placement services, college counseling, or "guaranteed exposure" packages
  • Accept advertising from programs, agencies, or recruiting services that would bias the model
  • Share athlete data with third parties without explicit consent
  • Inflate scores for premium subscribers or partner programs
  • Build a ranking that treats potential as synonymous with pedigree

These six constraints are logged in the codebase, the roadmap, and this page. If you ever see FieldCheck violating one of them, that's the moment to stop trusting it.

What makes the
model different.

  • Five-layer scoring across every sport — Physical, Mindset, Production, Defense, Pathway. Not a composite ranking system. A shape.
  • Opportunity-context adjustment — a walk-on from rural Mississippi with equal production to a 5-star California recruit is a different story. The model accounts for it.
  • Longitudinal trajectory — scores accumulate over time. A player trending from 7.5 to 8.8 over two seasons is more interesting than a static 8.5 with no movement.
  • Athlete voice layer — athletes can submit their own context. Not to override the model — to add what the public record can't see.
  • Calibration-first — 36 case anchors, 88% accuracy on verified historical outcomes, 96% accuracy when the model diverges from consensus. The calibration dashboard is public.
  • Honest caveats — every verdict surfaces what the model doesn't know. Thin data is labeled thin data, not dressed up as insight.

Who built it.

TDH
ThinkDifferent Holdings — a solo-founder AI product studio. FieldCheck is one of several tools in the XCheck portfolio built around a single thesis: trust layers where retail loses to information asymmetry. EdgeCheck, FieldCheck, RentCheck, SchoolCheck. Same framework. Different markets.

The moat is the data layer, the calibration corpus, the scoring philosophy, and the six constraints above. The code is open to examination. The calibration is public. The methodology is documented.
