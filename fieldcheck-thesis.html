01 · The Problem

Eight million amateurs. One question. Nobody solving it.

"I don't know how good I really am, where I rank, what I need to do to get noticed by colleges — and every service that claims to tell me costs money my family doesn't have, or is obviously rigged." — The amateur athlete, every day, in every sport, in every state

This is the central problem in amateur athletics. It is one sentence, and the entire industry has chosen not to solve it.

Eight million US high school athletes live this every day. Twenty-five million parents share the anxiety. Five hundred thousand high school and club coaches work without an objective verdict layer. Tens of thousands of scouts, recruiting coordinators, and NIL agents rely on services they know are gameable. The market is enormous, the pain is total, and the existing solutions are not solving the problem they claim to solve.

The recruiting media tier (247Sports, Rivals, On3) is structurally incentivized to keep its methodology opaque — opacity is the product. Their flagship outputs (composite rankings, NIL valuations) have been documented as pay-to-play and have suffered methodology rewrites and public retractions inside the last twelve months. The training/tape tier (Hudl, MaxPreps) provides workflow, but produces no decision-grade verdict. The conversion-funnel tier (NCSA, FieldLevel, SportsRecruits, CaptainU) is sales operations dressed as software — optimized for converting anxious parents into memberships, not for telling them the truth.

FieldCheck IQ exists to do the one thing no incumbent will: publish a free, methodologically open, sport-agnostic, verdict-grade evaluation for every amateur athlete, in every sport, at every level — and show our work.

02 · The Gap

Six broken layers. Each broken structurally, not by accident.

Every existing layer of the amateur athletics stack is broken in a structurally different way. None of them are coincidence. Each is a logical consequence of the business model behind it. Sunlight does not fix any of them — sunlight kills all of them.

01 · Recruiting media

Opacity is the product

247Sports, Rivals, On3. Composite rankings sold as proprietary content. Opacity is the revenue model — if they publish methodology, they stop selling subscriptions. Pay-to-play has been documented for years. The On3 Trentyn Flowers December 2025 retraction is one of many cases where speed and engagement incentives broke audit discipline.
02 · Conversion sales

Anxious parents, not data

NCSA, FieldLevel, SportsRecruits, CaptainU. Software wrapped around sales operations. 1,100+ "experts" converting anxious parents into MVP+ memberships at $1,000+/year. Unit economics depend on hope and effort, not on athlete decision-quality. The "ranking" is whatever helps the upsell.
03 · Stats only

Numbers without verdict

MaxPreps. Box scores, schedules, season-aggregate stats. No synthesis. No shape. No path. Tells you how many points a player scored last week. Does not tell you what tier they belong to, where they're heading, or what they need to improve. Useful as one of our sixteen input voices — not a competitor.
04 · Tape only

Workflow without intelligence

Hudl. The platform of record for tape and game film. Built for coaches' workflow — never built an athlete-facing verdict product. Hudl IQ exists, but is football-only, coach-only, and B2B. The athlete cannot see themselves through it.
05 · Profile pages

LinkedIn for recruiting

FieldLevel, SportsRecruits, CaptainU. Static profile pages, search filters, contact-the-coach buttons. Utility software with no moat. The athlete fills in their own data and hopes a coach finds it. No verdict. No reasoning. No truth.
06 · Internal scouting

Private, never productized

College programs and pro franchises run private scouting departments with proprietary grading. None of it is productized for amateurs. The athlete being graded never sees the grade. The system is closed by definition.

Stack these six layers and one structural feature emerges: every existing incumbent is unable to become the product the market actually needs. Their business models prevent the pivot. Their incentives erode the discipline. Their installed bases punish the rewrite. This is not a market with strong incumbents we have to displace — it is a market with structurally compromised incumbents we have to walk past.

04 · Methodology

Eight facets. Sixteen voices. Six constraints. Public.

Every FieldCheck IQ verdict is produced by a single algorithm, the same across every sport and every level. The algorithm has three pillars: a framework of eight facets, a synthesis across sixteen analytical voices, and six structural constraints that govern what the product is allowed to do.

04.1 The 8 canonical facets

Every athlete in the corpus is evaluated against the same eight facets. The facets are designed to capture what coaching staffs and scouts already evaluate — formalized, normalized, and made public.

Facet 01
Character
Integrity, off-field conduct, work ethic, accountability, leadership outside the lines.
Facet 02
Mindset
Competitive drive, fearlessness, internal belief, willingness to take the moment.
Facet 03
Mental Strength
Resilience under pressure, response to adversity, ability to reset, composure in late-game moments.
Facet 04
Talent
Natural ability, technical ceiling, position-specific skill, the things you cannot teach.
Facet 05
Physical
Size, athleticism, strength, durability, length, explosiveness, recovery profile.
Facet 06
Mental / IQ
Court / field awareness, anticipation, decision speed, game intelligence, situational reads.
Facet 07
Coachability
Response to coaching, growth rate, team fit, capacity to absorb correction and improve.
Facet 08
Competitiveness
Refusal to lose, killer instinct, response to elite opposition, on-field intensity.

Every facet is scored on a 1–10 scale. The composite (FieldCheck IQ) is the synthesized result across all eight, weighted by sport and position. The polygon visualization on every verdict page is the athlete's unique fingerprint across the eight facets.

04.2 The 16 analytical voices

Every verdict is produced by reading the athlete through sixteen analytical voices — the perspectives a real coaching staff already weighs. Each voice contributes evidence, confidence, and explicit dissent if it disagrees with the synthesis.

The synthesis is Bayesian — voices update prior beliefs with new evidence, and disagreement between voices is preserved (not averaged away). Dissent is published on the verdict page alongside consensus. Every reader can see why a composite is what it is, what the voices agreed on, and where they fought.

04.3 The 6 structural constraints

Every product decision is bound by six constraints. These are not features. They are guardrails — what FieldCheck IQ is forbidden from doing, because doing those things would compromise the verdict layer the market needs.

  1. Free forever for athletes. Every verdict, every facet, every polygon, every trajectory, every peer-compare, every share card is free for athletes and their families. Forever. No paywall on truth.
  2. No pay-to-rank. No athlete, agent, parent, or program can pay to be ranked higher. Composite scores cannot be purchased. Tier flips cannot be funded.
  3. Public methodology. The eight facets are public. The sixteen voices are public. The synthesis rules are public. Anyone can audit a verdict back to its sources.
  4. One algorithm, every athlete. The same algorithm runs across every player, every sport, every level. No special-casing. No private grades for marquee athletes. The math is the math.
  5. Sources cited on every verdict. Every verdict page links to the evidence the synthesis drew from. Readers can disagree with the synthesis — they cannot dispute the inputs.
  6. Dissent published, not averaged. When the sixteen voices disagree, we publish the disagreement. The reader sees consensus and dispute. The synthesis is honest about what it does not know.
// The unifying principle Every constraint above is something the incumbents cannot commit to without burning down their revenue model. That asymmetry — what is impossible for them is foundational for us — is the methodology moat.

  • The window closes when an incumbent buys their way in. Hudl, ESPN, or a private-equity buyer of On3 could fund a copy in 2027. The window to become the brand-of-record for amateur intelligence is now, not next year. Whoever owns the brand in December 2026 owns it forever.
