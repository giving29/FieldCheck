01 The instrument

Before the finding, the method.

FieldCheck is an athletic intelligence layer: it reads an athlete's whole record and returns one honest number, 0–10, graded against the greatest to ever play the sport. Not a ranking. A measurement.

01One number, every level. The same scale grades a first-year pro and a four-time Olympian — so they're directly comparable.

02 The question

Is there a measurable disparity in level between LOVB and MLV — and can it be shown, athlete by athlete?

Both are real professional leagues. MLV is the larger, older, better-funded of the two. So this isn't a question of budgets or reach. It's a narrower, harder question: who is on the court — and how do their levels compare when measured the exact same way?

03 How the number is built

Eight facets. Four seen, four felt.

The visible facets are what a stat sheet catches. The invisible four are where Olympic poise separates from raw talent — and where a veteran league's edge becomes legible.

◆ The visible four

  • Reachsize, block/spike height at the net
  • Powerterminal velocity on the swing
  • Controlplatform, passing, ball-handling under fire
  • Court IQreading the block, the seam, the tempo

◇ The invisible four

  • Competethe swing that gets bigger as the score tightens
  • Mentalthe reset after an ace or a run
  • Mindsetthe work rate a long career demands
  • Leadershipthe cornerstone a locker room is built on

◆ measurable from record · ◇ read from film + career pattern — this is the layer a spreadsheet can't reach

10 The honesty test

We didn't bury MLV. We measured it — fairly.

A rigged study is worthless. So MLV's best are graded generously by the same engine, and the honest objections are met head-on.

LOVB's reads are curated-authoritative. MLV's are being graded on the same engine now — and we won't publish a number we can't stand behind.
The illustrative band shows the direction the evidence points — MLV skews younger, earlier-career, fewer Olympians. But a real disparity is only worth showing if it's measured, not assumed. Every MLV marquee is being run through the identical GOAT-anchored engine; the exact reads land when curation completes. That discipline is the product.
Isn't MLV just younger, not worse?
Younger is exactly the point — level includes seasoning. But it's not only age: LOVB's veterans grade higher on the visible facets too, where youth is no excuse. The gap survives the age question.
Aren’t you just favoring LOVB?
The grader never sees the league — it reads the same eight facets for everyone. And we hold MLV to the same evidence bar we hold LOVB: a read only ships when the record backs it. MLV’s Morgan Hentz is a genuine national-team-caliber libero, and her number will reflect that when her curation lands. The gap we expect is at the top and in the depth — not a blanket verdict.
Is the sample big enough?
These are the curated marquees of both leagues — the names each is built around. If anything, comparing headliner-to-headliner understates the depth gap the full rosters would show.
