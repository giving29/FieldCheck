FAQ.

The questions people actually ask, answered honestly. No marketing language. If we don't know, we say so.

Why is Boozer only a 7.4 STAR when 247 has him as #1?
Amateur cap doctrine. No college freshman can be ELITE (>7.5) at FieldCheck until they've proven against pro defenders. That's a deliberate honesty about ceiling visibility. Boozer is graded against college peers, not against the projected NBA him.
Are these predictions or rankings?
Both, sort of. The composite + tier is our current grade. The 50 sealed predictions (sealed May 17 2026, check-in Aug 15 2028) are forward-looking statements with confidence intervals. The grade is what we know now; the prediction is what we're betting on.
What sports do you cover?
Currently: men's basketball (82), football (56), women's basketball (34), women's volleyball (24), baseball (18), men's volleyball (9), soccer M+W (7). See the coverage map for the full breakdown. Hockey, tennis, and track are queued for Q3 2026.
Who's behind the verdicts?
Solo founder + AI loop. The 25-PhD panel reviewed the framework and the first cohort verdicts. Specific names anonymized publicly for now; PhD credentials are real and verifiable on request.
How is this different from 247sports / On3 / Rivals?
(1) Same framework across every sport — basketball, football, volleyball all measured on the same 8 facets, just with different weights. (2) Pre-registered predictions with check-ins. (3) Honest amateur caps. (4) Source citations on every claim. (5) We publish our misses.
Why is the data sometimes thin?
If a facet doesn't have 3 independent sources, it's marked partial. We don't fake confidence. A 6.2 PROSPECT with all 8 facets fully sourced is more reliable than a 7.4 STAR with only 5 facets triangulated. See coverage map for known gaps.
How often are verdicts updated?
Monthly for the curated marquees (PhD round). Quarterly for the full cohort (weight recalibration). Major framework versions every 5 years (v2.0 targeted 2028-2030).
Can I see my own kid evaluated?
Cohort requests via the contact form on any verdict page. The PhD panel batches requests monthly. We're intentional about not turning into a pay-for-grade service — that breaks the prediction-ledger contract.
Is FieldCheck pro-athletes or pro-public?
Pro-public. Every verdict is free forever for everyone. The future B2B/program tier (Phase 14, late 2026) charges programs that want bulk roster eval and trajectory monitoring. Public-facing site stays free.
What if an athlete or family disagrees with a verdict?
Dispute via contact form. The panel will review with the new evidence and issue a versioned re-grade if warranted. Re-grades are public and versioned. No silent revisions. The dissent goes on the record either way.
How can I trust the photos?
The photo cascade pulls from school athletic sites, 247sports, On3, MaxPreps, ESPN, NBA.com, and Wikipedia in priority order. Each verdict page shows source attribution on hover. Multi-source doctrine applies to photos same as it applies to grades.
Are you affiliated with any school, league, or agent?
No. Solo-founder-plus-AI. No affiliations, no sponsored content, no pay-for-placement. The honest version of evaluation.
