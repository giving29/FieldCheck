01 · The Vision

One sentence. No noise.

FieldCheck is the trust layer for athlete decisions where retail loses to information asymmetry.

Every word is load-bearing. Most strategy documents pad their thesis with adjectives. Ours does not, because the operating principle of the company is that every word, every Voice attribution, every reasoning trace, every visible asset must survive scrutiny on first read.

Trust layer
Not a recruiting service. Not a media company. Not a ratings aggregator. Infrastructure that lives underneath every consequential choice an athlete and their family make.
Athlete decisions
Narrow on purpose. We exist at the moment of commitment — the school choice, the transfer, the NIL deal, the pro entry. We do not do general sports content.
Retail loses
The asymmetry runs against families and athletes. The industry profits from keeping them in the dark. We invert that.
Information asymmetry
The moat. Same shape as the XCheck portfolio thesis. Same operating logic across every product we ship.
02 · The Strategic Premise

Three lies the recruiting industry survives on.

Every incumbent's revenue model depends on at least one of these holding up. None of them survive scrutiny.

"Rankings are signal."
They're not. Rankings are inventory. Recruiting services need a constantly-refreshing top 25, top 100, top 247, top 300 to monetize subscription access and ad inventory. The ranking itself is the product, not the prediction.
"Access is value."
No. Access is a paywall around public-record information — Hudl tape, AAU box scores, regional all-star rosters, NCAA APR data — repackaged as proprietary. The premium tier sells you the right to see what should have been free.
"Opacity benefits all parties."
It doesn't. Opacity benefits only the seller — schools, coaches, recruiting services. Athletes and families have always been the unpaid suppliers of trust to a market that uses their decisions as content. We exist to invert that.

Every product decision at FieldCheck starts by asking which lie we are most directly disproving in that release. If we cannot name it, we don't ship.

03 · The Three Layers

Voices. Brains. Surface.

The architecture beneath the product. Each layer is independently defensible. Together, they are uncopyable.

01
Trust Engine
The Voices.
Sixteen real people per sport. Players, parents, coaches, scouts — whose questions and friction drive every product decision. Their lived experience is the methodology. Not algorithms pretending to be objective. Real voices saying "this is what I needed and never got." Voice attribution is non-negotiable: if we can't name which Voice asked for it, we don't ship it.
02
Execution Engine
The Operating Brains.
CMO, CFO, Data, COO — four specialist agents working in concert per-decision, never as a standing committee. They translate Voice friction into shippable product. Lightspeed, customer-obsessed, zero bureaucracy. Apple has no committees; we have no committees.
03
Product Surface
Verdicts. Profiles. Voice Notes. Position-Fit.
Every artifact a family touches. Every verdict cites a Voice. Every prediction has a reasoning trace. Every visible asset meets the Apple-grade bar — measurable, not vibes. The surface is the only thing the user sees, so the surface is the only thing that gets judged.

05 · The Competitive Frame

We win the games we choose to play.

We do not compete on the dimensions incumbents have already won. We compete on the dimensions that make their wins irrelevant.

We do not compete on

  • Volume of profiles. Incumbents win this — and lose families anyway.
  • Speed of news. Twitter wins this and we don't need to.
  • Insider access. They sell relationships; we sell independence.

We compete on

  • Accuracy. The prediction was right, traceable, and auditable.
  • Honest scope. What we don't know is explicit, not hidden.
  • Independence. No school-paid placements. No collective sponsorships. No recruiting-service relationships. Ever.
  • Apple-grade craft. The verdict feels like it was made for one family, not scraped for all of them.
The incumbents cannot copy this without burning down their business model. That is precisely the point. — Strategic Premise
06 · The Gold Standard

FieldCheck Tier. The language the industry adopts.

A ranking is a position on a list. A FieldCheck Tier is a position in a frame. The difference is the difference between noise and standard.

We do not rank athletes. Rankings are inventory and we are not in the inventory business. Instead, we maintain a four-band classification system — anchored to outcome data, calibrated against Voice-verified rubrics, defended by a public methodology paper that anyone can read, dispute, or cite.

ICON
The rare apex. Generational. Burrow. Madisen Skinner. The athlete whose ceiling redefines what the position can be. Designation is conferred sparingly and conservatively.
ELITE
Top of class, multi-year sustained. Pro pipeline credible. Conference-shaping. Multiple Voice-verified signals of trajectory.
STAR
Regional or conference apex. College starter at minimum, pro plausible. The bedrock of any serious roster.
PROSPECT
Talent and trajectory present, ceiling not yet locked. Watch this name. The earliest defensible designation in the system.

Why this is different

A ranking says "she's #47." That number is noise. It changes every week. It serves the publisher, not the family.

A FieldCheck Tier says: "She is ELITE because of these five Voice-verified signals, traceable to these public-record sources, with this confidence interval, anchored to a comp library of twelve athletes who tested the same and went these places." It is not a position on a list. It is a position in a frame.

The frame is published. Every threshold, every dimension, every Voice attribution is in the methodology paper. The paper is the standard. The standard is the moat.

See also: The FieldCheck Tier · A Public Methodology · FC-METH-001 v1.0 — the formal spec paper accompanying this framework.

07 · The Three List Formats

FC100. Greatness Path. Position-Fit Apex.

Three named publications. Apple-keynote energy, not ESPN Top 25 churn. Each one performs a specific strategic function and is released at a distinct cadence.

Annual · Flagship

The FieldCheck 100

"The Time 100. The Fortune 500. The FieldCheck 100."

An annual cohort of one hundred athletes, drawn across all sports, classified by FieldCheck Tier. Clustered in tiers, not ranked 1 through 100. The 2026 edition might publish as "the 8 ICONs of 2026, the 32 ELITEs, the 60 STARs." Released once per year on a fixed date that the industry learns to anticipate.

The strategic function: become the citation everyone has to acknowledge. Major media will write about who made it and who didn't. Coaches will reference it in living-room pitches. The FC100 is the prestige surface that drags the methodology into the cultural foreground.

Sub-segments will emerge over time within FC100 — by sport, by position, by region — but the flagship release stays one cohort, one cover, one cadence.

Ongoing · Designation

The Greatness Path

"A status that travels with the athlete."

Not a list. A named cohort that an athlete is invited into when their FieldCheck Tier and trajectory cross a threshold. "She is on the Greatness Path" becomes a FieldCheck-issued designation that travels with the athlete from high school through college through pro.

The strategic function: standard adoption. When a coach quotes "Greatness Path," the coach is citing FieldCheck. When a media outlet writes about an athlete being on the Greatness Path, the outlet is acknowledging the system. The designation does the standard-building work for us.

Sport-Specific · Reference

Position-Fit Apex

"The named apex case at each tier in each position."

For every major position in every major sport, the canonical apex case at each FieldCheck Tier. "Madisen Skinner is the W-VB OH ICON for 2025." "Joe Burrow is the QB ICON for the 2018–2019 trajectory class." Reference architecture, not a leaderboard.

The strategic function: position-fit becomes the language coaches use to explain to a recruit what tier they are projecting toward. When a coach says "we think you can be the next position-fit STAR at OH," the coach is using FieldCheck terminology to set athlete expectations. The methodology embeds itself in the recruiting conversation.

09 · What We Are Not

The temptations we refuse.

When the company is doing well, every adjacent market will look like growth. The strategic discipline is naming what we will not become, in advance, so the answer is reflexive.

  • We are not a recruiting service.
  • We are not a media company.
  • We are not a ratings aggregator.
  • We are not a sports betting product.
  • We are not building general consumer sports content.

When we are tempted to be any of these, the answer is no. The temptation will arrive disguised as opportunity. We name the lines in advance precisely so we can recognize the temptation when it arrives.

10 · True North

Standard adoption. Nothing else.

User count is a vanity metric. Subscription revenue is a downstream metric. Standard adoption is the metric that, if it goes up, makes everything else go up.

Every two weeks, we measure the Standard Adoption Index. Every appearance of FieldCheck Tier language in a coach quote, a recruiting service write-up, a major-media article, a college program internal document. Each one is a data point. The graph that matters is the cumulative count of those data points over time.

When that graph goes vertical — when "FieldCheck Tier" appears in a SportsCenter chyron without prompting, when an Alabama position coach uses the language in a Tuesday recruiting pitch, when a New York Times article opens with a FieldCheck ICON designation — the strategic battle is over.

Everything else we build is in service of that one number going up.

// The Five Rules

What wins this game. What loses it.

  1. Independence stays absolute. The first school-paid placement is the end. There is no exception, no "limited test," no "just this one."
  2. Voices stay real. Every Voice is a real person, not a persona. The moment we synthesize a Voice, we are indistinguishable from the incumbents.
  3. Verdicts stay auditable. Every prediction has a reasoning trace and a Voice attribution. Black-box AI verdicts are exactly what the incumbents do.
  4. Craft stays Apple-grade. Every visible asset, every interaction, every word. Measurable bar, not vibes. If we cannot measure it, we cannot claim it.
  5. Speed stays lightspeed. Zero bureaucracy, zero committees, zero politics. If you slow us down, you are done.
