The Gap

Five layers of sports tech exist.
The judgment layer does not.

Billions of dollars now price young athletes - NIL deals, transfer portal decisions, roster spend. Every one of those prices rests on an evaluation. And the evaluation layer is opaque, unaccountable, two sports deep, and ignores 99 percent of athletes. That is the gap.

01 - The Market Forces

NIL turned every evaluation into a financial event.

Since 2021, college sports has become a structured talent economy. The NIL market reached roughly $2.3 billion in 2024-25, growing toward $2.5B+ with the House Settlement institutionalizing direct revenue sharing. Over 40 states now permit high school NIL. Athletic departments make recruiting decisions that carry six and seven figure consequences per athlete.

Every market that prices assets eventually builds a ratings layer - equities got S&P, credit got FICO, bonds got Moody's. The athlete economy is pricing teenagers with no accountable rating infrastructure at all. The question every family, coach, collective, and GM now asks - "what is this athlete actually worth, and why?" - has no honest answer on the market.

The evidence is on their own pages.

247Sports states its system plainly: 32 five-stars to mirror 32 first-round NFL picks. The entire apparatus is a pro-draft projection for the top of two sports - not an evaluation framework for athletes. A D3 point guard, a JUCO pitcher, a volleyball outside hitter: structurally invisible, forever.

And the structural reason incumbents cannot close the gap: their business models forbid it. Media companies (247, On3, Rivals) sell access and fan hope - public accountability for wrong calls destroys subscription value. Hudl sells to teams and coaches - publicly grading those coaches' athletes against each other threatens its own customers. The judgment layer can only be built by someone whose entire product IS the accountable judgment.
