§ The scaffold sport · Coming next

Football — structure in place.

The Pathway tool already shows a football tab (currently disabled). The position taxonomy is drafted. Archetypes will anchor on visible college and pro careers. Same independence stance: no school payments, walk-away default, public-record sourcing.

Status Scaffold
§ I · Position taxonomy drafted

11 positions, 3 sides of the ball.

Offense
QB · RB · WR · TE · OL
Five position groups on offense. Each gets archetype clusters anchored on visible college careers (Heisman finalists, AP All-Americans, NFL combine performers).
Defense
DL · LB · CB · S
Four position groups on defense. Combine measurables matter more here; we'll need a stronger data layer for sub-second 40 / vertical / shuttle thresholds.
Special teams
K · P · LS
Three specialist roles. Smaller athlete pool; archetype matching becomes more individual than statistical.

§ The queued sport · Behind football

Basketball — in the queue.

Basketball follows football in the build queue. Both men's and women's basketball have well-developed public data layers — NCAA, WNBA, NBA, EuroLeague — so the platform extension is mostly an exercise in taxonomy mapping.

Status Queued
§ I · What this will look like

Both men's and women's together.

Anchor leagues
NCAA · WNBA · NBA
Both college and pro pathways. Unrestricted Free Agent and transfer portal flow tracked. International prospects (EuroLeague, Spanish ACB, Australian NBL) included.
Positions
PG · SG · SF · PF · C
Five traditional positions plus modern hybrids (combo guards, stretch fours, point forwards). Modern basketball is increasingly position-fluid.
Physical priors
Wingspan · vertical · sprint
More measurable than football. Wingspan is the strongest single physical predictor. NBA Combine data is public for elite prospects.
Pipeline alternatives
G League · overseas · NIL
Unlike volleyball, basketball has multiple viable professional pathways at varying compensation levels. We'll surface all of them honestly.
Basketball is behind football in the queue. Realistic timeline: late 2026 or early 2027. Get notified when it ships.
§ The queued sport · Lower priority

Soccer — if it's needed.

Soccer queues lowest among the four. The Angel City / NWSL ecosystem is already well-served by existing data layers and journalism. We'll build soccer only if a credible gap appears that we can fill independently.

Status Queued
§ I · Why later

The ecosystem is already strong.

Existing coverage
NWSL · journalism · data
Angel City FC, Bay FC, NWSL broadly already have strong public data layers, dedicated journalism, and analytics services. Less obvious gap to fill.
Global pyramid
Complex · multi-tier
Soccer's global tier pyramid (Premier League down to non-league) is harder to map cleanly than the bounded U.S. college-to-pro structure we use for volleyball, football, basketball.
When it makes sense
U.S. youth-to-NWSL
If we ever build soccer, it'll focus on U.S. youth-to-NWSL pathway specifically — closest structural parallel to LOVB. ECNL → DA → college → NWSL.
Reason to revisit
A specific ecosystem gap
We'll revisit if a specific use case emerges — a parent need, a club need, a league need — that current tools don't serve.

Why multi-sport scaffold visible from day one? Because the platform's value is the capability layer pattern, not the sport. Volleyball is the deepest first execution. Football comes next. Basketball after that. Soccer if and when there's a real gap.
