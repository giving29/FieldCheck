Confidence intervals · interpretation

Each prediction includes a p-value indicating the percentile confidence (e.g. p70 = we expect the actual to land inside the stated CI 70% of the time). Predictions with lower p-values (p55-p60) are riskier calls — usually high-variance amateurs whose ceiling we can't yet observe in pro contexts.

What "wrong" looks like

If an athlete's actual 2028 composite lands outside the predicted CI, that prediction is logged as a miss. The miss appears on this page with:

  • Athlete name, sealed grade, sealed CI
  • Actual observed composite + tier
  • Delta + direction (over/under)
  • Brief post-mortem: which facet drifted? what evidence did we miss?

We don't retract. We don't paywall the miss page. We don't reframe the prediction in hindsight. That's the contract.
