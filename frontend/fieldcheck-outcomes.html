Hits · where doctrine caught the signal

Cases where the 8-facet framework would have surfaced a non-consensus call that turned out right.
Nikola Jokić 2014 NBA Draft · #41 overall Hit · doctrine surfaced the gem
Consensus then
Late-second-round flyer. Heavy international. Poor athleticism. Most boards had him outside the first round.
Doctrine read
High Mental/IQ (court vision, passing reads). High Coachability (refined skill arc through age 17-19). Modest Physical but compensated by elite Talent ceiling in skill-shooting. Composite likely 7.0-7.4 STAR even with Physical drag.
What happened
3x MVP (2021, 2022, 2024). 2023 NBA champion + Finals MVP. ICON tier (9.5+) achieved.
Lesson
Heavy Physical weighting common in NBA scouting at the time would have flagged him low. Multi-source first + Mental/IQ weighting would have caught him. Validates the doctrine's resistance to single-facet over-weighting.
Giannis Antetokounmpo 2013 NBA Draft · #15 overall Hit · ceiling read correct
Consensus then
Greek B-league teenager. Unknown ceiling. Most teams couldn't watch him play live. Talent evaluators uncertain.
Doctrine read
Elite Physical (7'+ wingspan still growing). Visible Coachability (rapid skill addition year over year). High Competitiveness in available footage. Limited multi-source - tier-target 7.0 STAR with explicit "ceiling uncapped if Physical projects."
What happened
2x MVP (2019, 2020). 2021 NBA champion + Finals MVP. ELITE+ trajectory by year 5.
Lesson
Even with thin source-depth, the Physical-trajectory signal was strong enough to override consensus uncertainty. Multi-source first doesn't mean "wait for consensus" - it means weight by what's actually verified.
Caitlin Clark 2020 HS recruiting class · #4 nationally Hit · scoring ceiling clear early
Consensus then
Top-5 recruit but UConn/Stanford passed. Iowa landed her - widely seen as a strong-but-not-program-changing get.
Doctrine read
Generational Talent in shooting range + creation. High Mental/IQ on basketball reads. HS amateur cap (5.4 SCOUT) would have applied — but explicit trajectory note: "Project to STAR by sophomore year, ELITE by junior."
What happened
NCAA all-time scoring leader. Two Final Fours. #1 WNBA pick 2024. Rookie of the Year. Generational impact on WBB visibility.
Lesson
The HS amateur cap (Tenet 53 era) keeps us honest at evaluation time but doesn't blind us to trajectory. Composite + trajectory notes do more than composite alone.
Luka Dončić 2018 NBA Draft · #3 overall Hit · tier confirmed
Consensus then
EuroLeague MVP at 19. Mock drafts ranged from #2 to #5. Some scouts worried about NBA athleticism translation.
Doctrine read
Elite Talent + elite Mental/IQ + proven against pro-level competition (Real Madrid + Slovenian senior national team). Adult composite achievable — projected 7.5-8.5 ELITE.
What happened
5x All-NBA, multiple MVP finalist appearances, Finals appearance 2024. ELITE confirmed.
Lesson
When the amateur cap doesn't apply (pro-level competition already overcome), the composite can run hot honestly. Doctrine's age-context awareness pays off.

Misses · where doctrine would have been wrong

Cases where the 8-facet framework would have likely been on the wrong side. We publish these.
Anthony Bennett 2013 NBA Draft · #1 overall Miss · doctrine would have rated too high
Consensus then
UNLV power forward. Surprise #1 pick. Multiple analysts skeptical but composite metrics looked strong.
Doctrine read (retrospective)
Strong Physical + adequate Talent + solid college production. Multi-source data was actually thin on Mindset and Competitiveness at the pro intensity level. Doctrine might have called him 6.5-7.0 PROSPECT-to-STAR.
What happened
52 career NBA games. Out of the league by age 24. Widely considered one of the worst #1 picks in NBA history.
Lesson
Mindset/Competitiveness facets are the hardest to source. When data is thin there, doctrine rules say "mark partial, expect downside." A more rigorous application of multi-source-first would have surfaced this. Codified in current doctrine: thin Mindset → automatic 0.5 composite drag.
Sam Bowie (vs. Michael Jordan) 1984 NBA Draft · #2 vs #3 Miss · doctrine in 1984 weighting
Consensus then
Portland took Bowie #2 over Jordan. Most boards had Bowie ahead due to size and "need" weighting.
Doctrine read (1984 weighting)
If Physical was weighted higher than it currently is, Bowie's 7'1" would have advantaged him at the era's "need a center" baseline. Even with Jordan's 8/8 facets, sport-specific weights mattered.
What happened
Jordan: 6x champion, 5x MVP, ICON of ICONs. Bowie: respectable career derailed by injuries.
Lesson
Sport-specific facet weights must be CONTEMPORARY. A 1984 weighting overemphasized size for centers; 2026 weighting recognizes wing-creator value. Doctrine evolves - and old weights are an admitted blind spot of the framework, not a permanent limitation.

What this teaches us

The framework's job isn't to be infallible. It's to be consistent, source-checkable, and honestly self-graded. A 4-for-6 retrospective ratio on contested cases is genuinely useful if (a) the misses are admitted and (b) the doctrine evolves from them. Both are non-negotiable. The Bennett case directly shaped our current rule that thin Mindset sourcing triggers a 0.5 composite drag. The Bowie case is why we revisit sport-specific facet weights annually.
