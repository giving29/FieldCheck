How we actually grade an athlete.

Nine pages of methodology condensed into one. No SEO fluff, no jargon-cloaked black box. The 8 facets, the source tiers, the PhD panel, the calibration loop, and the predictions we sealed last month for an August 2028 check-in.

The 8 canonical facets

One framework. Same 8 facets across every sport, every level. Different evidence weights per sport, but the structure is invariant. Calibration changes are versioned and dated; every shipped marquee notes which version graded it.

F1

Character

How the athlete behaves under pressure when no one's keeping score. Verifiable through public-record signals (locker-room incidents, team conduct, on-field decisions in low-stakes moments).

F2

Mindset

Stated and demonstrated approach to growth, failure, and identity. Pulls from interviews, post-game quotes, social posts, coaches' public framing.

F3

Mental Strength

Resilience in adversity. Comebacks. Performance after losses or injuries. Composure in pressure spots (final minutes, championship games).

F4

Talent

The natural ceiling that scouting eyes have been describing for a decade. Skill arcs, raw measurables that don't show up in stat sheets.

F5

Physical

Measurables relative to sport + position. Calibrated to roster medians at the target pro tier (LOVB for womens-vball, NBA for mens-bball, etc.).

F6

Mental / IQ

Game IQ. Reads. Decision quality. The "feel" component. Captured via possession-level analysis, post-game decision audits.

F7

Coachability

Listens, adjusts, demonstrably improves between seasons. Triangulates through coach quotes + observable in-game changes.

F8

Competitiveness

Doesn't take possessions off. Shows up in losses. Demonstrable in level-of-play, not just stat sheets. The "wants it" axis.

The tier ladder

Composites map to tiers via fixed thresholds — same for every sport. Bright lines deliberately chosen to be slightly harder than market consensus, so a STAR at FieldCheck means more than 5-star at On3 or 247.

Why the amateur caps? HS amateurs cap at SCOUT (5.4). They can't be STAR until they prove against college defenders. College freshmen cap at STAR (7.4). They can't be ELITE until they prove against pro athletes. These are honest constraints, not pessimism — they protect against the recurring market mistake of grading off potential too early.

The PhD panel

25 named PhDs (anonymized publicly for now) reviewed the framework and the first cohort's verdicts. 1 documented dissent (Vance on Flagg) is on the record. The same panel reviews on a rolling schedule:

  • Weekly: Patel reads new verdicts as they ship, flags drift
  • Monthly: Full PhD round — score audit, framework edge cases
  • Quarterly: Weight updates to the facet system, calibration overlays
  • Annual: Refinements ship as v1.x
  • 5-year: Major version (v2.0 targeted 2028-2030)

Data sources (many, never one)

The doctrine: for any data type, default to many sources with a priority cascade. Wikipedia is one of many, not the default.

  • School sitesOfficial college athletic department rosters (Sidearm Sports CDN for ~90% of D1 programs). Verified headshots, official bios, class year, hometown.
  • 247sportsRecruit profiles for HS athletes. Industry rankings, position analysis, evaluation copy.
  • On3 / RivalsCross-validation of recruit rankings. Different methodology, different blind spots.
  • MaxPrepsHigh school stats and game logs. Single-source-of-truth for HS verifiable production.
  • ESPN / NBA / NFL CDNsPro-level profiles, headshots, official stats endpoints.
  • CFBDCollege Football Data — roster, recruits, transfer portal, official rankings.
  • Sports ReferenceHistorical stats across all major sports. The ground truth for season-by-season production.
  • WikipediaBio context, career arcs, post-career trajectories. Last in the cascade, not the default.
  • Public news + pressCoach quotes, post-game interviews, behavioral observations. Tier 3 source (must triangulate).
  • Curated PhD priorsFor ICONs and historical anchors, the panel's analytical record fills gaps that public-record alone can't.

What we don't use

  • Paywalled scouting reports we can't replicate. We don't take them; they can't be reverse-engineered for the panel.
  • Combine-only measurables in isolation. 40-yard times don't grade competitiveness.
  • Social media virality as a signal. Talent ≠ following.
  • NIL value as a talent input. (We track NIL separately as market output, not skill input.)
  • Anonymous source quotes that can't be triangulated.

The honest unknowns

Every verdict page surfaces "honest caveats" — what we can't know with the source set available. If we don't have 3 independent signals on a facet, the facet is marked partial. That's a feature, not a bug. A 6.2 PROSPECT with all facets fully sourced is more reliable than a 7.4 STAR where only 5 facets are triangulated.

Versioning + drift

Every grade is stamped with the framework version that scored it (currently v1.7.A). Calibration changes are versioned (V5.5 doctrine locked June 8 2026). When a v1.8 or v2.0 ships, all prior verdicts are re-scored and the deltas published. No silent revisions.

Multi-source first · Tenet 53 The methodology above isn't just for grading. It's the same pattern for every other data lookup — photos, stats, news, NIL. Cascade through many sources. Cache. First success wins. Never bet on one source. This is the lesson that took the longest to land.
