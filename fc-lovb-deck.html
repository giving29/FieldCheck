// Why we built this
The problem

Volleyball doesn't have a trust problem. It has a density problem.

Other XCheck verticals — rental, employment, healthcare — fight information asymmetry through skepticism. Volleyball doesn't need skepticism. It needs articulation. Pathways are opaque. The pro league is young. Founding-athlete stories are dispersed across press releases. 90 junior clubs sprawl without a central map. We built the map.

The positioning

Not an audit. A capability multiplier.

LOVB itself becomes stronger by having an articulate independent ecosystem around it.

// Independence guardrails
The seven principles

Independence, in writing.

1
Public-record sourcing. Every claim cites a LOVB page, named publication, or registry.
2
CI on every score. Confidence intervals always visible. Honest uncertainty over inflated confidence.
3
No school money. No college, club, or HS pays to be featured. We don't rank.
4
No affiliate fees. No commercial relationship with services, agents, or coaching certs.
5
Walk-away default. Every recommendation supports declining as a valid choice.
6
LOVB language stays LOVB's. "Founding Athlete," "Whole Athlete," "Coach of Coaches" — we cite, don't claim.
7
Athletes own their profiles. Any athlete can claim and edit via verified flow.

// LOVB relationship
The relationship with LOVB

Independent in money. Aligned in mission.

FieldCheck-LOVB is not commercially affiliated with League One Volleyball. No sponsorship, no partnership, no advertising relationship, no content licensing. We're not a child of LOVB.

We are aligned. LOVB's success matters because the broader U.S. women's volleyball ecosystem needs the league it built. If LOVB gets stronger because the data ecosystem around it gets stronger, that's a good outcome — for them, for us, for everyone. We are cousins, not children.

// Why this is defensible
Why this is defensible

Execution speed × independence discipline.

The platform's moat is the combination of (1) AI-tooled execution speed — 37 surfaces shipped in eight weeks — and (2) operational independence discipline — seven hard guardrails, public source manifest, no commercial dependencies. Neither alone is the moat. Together, they are.

An incumbent recruiting service can't replicate this because their commercial model demands the very dependencies we forbid. An AI-tooled new entrant can't replicate this because they would have to spend 18 months building the source-manifest discipline that already exists here. The moat is the intersection.
