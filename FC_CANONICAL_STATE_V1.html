▌ A · V5 algorithm philosophy · the gold standard
  • One number, one ceiling. Single 0-10 score per athlete. 10 = theoretical perfection, unreachable by any human.
  • Calculated from evidence. Each of the 8 facets scored vs theoretical-perfect-facet performance. No stages, no tiers, no buckets, no clamps.
  • Evidence rigor is the limiter. An HS top recruit naturally lands ~5 because the evidence is HS-only — not because of a cap. Pro-context facets can't score high without pro-context evidence.
  • Phenoms emerge naturally. Kobe at 17, LeBron at 17, KD at 18, Tiger at 17 score higher than peers because their evidence already shows pro-tier production, athleticism, competitive context. The algorithm doesn't suppress them — it correctly reads the evidence.
  • UTR-inspired. Single universal scale, gender/age/sport-blind. UTR doesn't have buckets — Sinner at 16 was already 12+ while normal 16-yo's are 5-7. The math produced that, not categories. We do the same.
  • Reference distribution, not enforcement. Jordan 9.9 · Duncan 9.3-9.6 · multi-MVPs 9.0-9.3 · top HS ~5 — these describe what evidence-driven scoring naturally produces. They are predicted outputs, not imposed ceilings.
"Making every number make sense and measured against the highest non-reachable ceiling of 10 — that's all there is."

04.3 The 6 structural constraints
Not features — guardrails. What FieldCheck IQ is forbidden from doing, because doing those things would compromise the verdict layer the market needs.
  1. Free forever for athletes. Every verdict, every facet, every polygon — free for athletes and families. Forever. No paywall on truth.
  2. No pay-to-rank. No athlete, agent, parent, or program can pay to be ranked higher. Composite scores cannot be purchased.
  3. Public methodology. Eight facets public. Sixteen voices public. Synthesis rules public. Anyone can audit a verdict back to its sources.
  4. One algorithm, every athlete. Same algorithm across every player, every sport, every level. No special-casing. No private grades for marquees.
  5. Sources cited on every verdict. Every verdict page links to the evidence. Readers can disagree with synthesis — not with inputs.
  6. Dissent published, not averaged. When voices disagree, we publish disagreement. The synthesis is honest about what it does not know.
// Unifying principle
Every constraint above is something the incumbents cannot commit to without burning their revenue model. That asymmetry — what is impossible for them is foundational for us — is the methodology moat.

Statistical Tests · Acceptance Criteria
  • 3.1 Paired t-test on per-player errors. For each Ri: H₁: E[error^baseline] > E[error^Ri]. Reject if p < 0.05 (one-sided).
  • 3.2 Holm-Bonferroni correction. Testing 6 refinements inflates Type I error. Test in order of expected effect size. Reject only those passing α / (k − rejected so far).
  • 3.3 Bootstrap CI on test/training ratio. Bootstrap 1000 random 70/30 splits. Acceptance: Mean ratio < 1.5 AND 95% CI upper bound < 2.0.
  • 3.4 Cumulative refinement test. Apply all approved refinements together. Target: Cumulative test RMSE < baseline test RMSE × 0.7 (30%+ reduction). If individual pass but cumulative fails → suspect interactions.
  • 3.5 Sport-specific subset tests. R4: NFL subset must improve significantly. R6: Soccer subset must not degrade.
  • 3.6 Tier-specific subset tests. R1-R4 targets bust prediction. Bust subset (n=8): current RMSE 1.18. Target with R1-R4: < 0.50.

Phenom doctrine · locked earned by evidence, not awarded by hype
A phenom is the rare athlete whose evidence at a sub-pro age already justifies higher scores than typical for their category. Requires ALL 4 criteria. If any one missing, athlete is not a phenom — scored against standard evidence ladder for category.
ReqRequirement
REQ 1Pro-tier evidence at sub-pro age. Not projection. Actual measurable pro-tier evidence demonstrated NOW. The evidence already exists; it's not a forecast.
REQ 2Multi-source corroboration. 3+ independent sources agree. Not 3 sources of the same recruiting service. Single-source phenom claims rejected.
REQ 3Older-competition validation. Dominance against significantly older competitors, not same-age cohort. Same-age dominance does NOT qualify.
REQ 4Sustained across multiple events. Pattern across multiple high-level events, not single-tournament dominance. Sample-of-one heroics do NOT qualify.

Anti-inflation doctrine · what does NOT count as evidence universal
These signals correlate with score inflation in V4 and prior. V5 explicitly excludes them from facet scoring. Score what the evidence shows NOW — not potential, projection, hype, or market expectation.
  • ▲ Recruiting rankings (247/On3/Rivals/ESPN) — these are projection consensus, not evidence. A 5-star ranking does not justify any specific facet score.
  • ▲ Draft slot / mock draft consensus — #1 overall pick projection is consensus expectation. Rookies do NOT inherit pro-tier scores by draft position.
  • ▲ NIL deal size — market price for marketing rights, not athletic facet performance. $8M NIL ≠ 8.0 facet score.
  • ▲ Media narrative / hype — opinion aggregates. Influence market consensus (the polygon shadow overlay) but do NOT score the FieldCheck polygon.
  • ▲ Family lineage — parent isn't the athlete. Lineage does not transfer evidence.
  • ▲ Coach quotes during recruitment — sales pitches. Post-arrival quotes count; recruitment quotes don't.
  • ▲ Single-event heroics — sample-of-one moments. Evidence must show pattern across multiple events at appropriate level.
  • ▲ Combine measurables without game translation — 40 time/vertical/wingspan alone are inputs to Physical facet but require game-level translation to score above median.
Default-low principle: when evidence is missing, the facet defaults to 2-3, NOT to average 5. The algorithm earns higher scores; it does not assume them. V4 assumed 5 baseline. V5 assumes low until proven. This is the core fix.
