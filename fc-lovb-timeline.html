The Timeline · LOVB chronology · 2020 → 2027

From idea to six markets, in seven years.

A chronological history of League One Volleyball — from 2020 founding through inaugural 2025 season, 2026 ownership consolidation, and 2027 three-city expansion. Major milestones only; every event sourced from lovb.com press releases or named publications.

2020 2022 2023 2024 2025 2026 2027
2020

The founding idea.

Co-founders Katlyn Gao and Lucy Watson Idehen formalize the vision: integrate the U.S. women's volleyball pipeline end-to-end — junior clubs through a professional league.

2020
FOUNDATION

LOVB Inc. founded.

Katlyn Gao (Co-CEO) and Lucy Watson Idehen (Co-CEO) found League One Volleyball Inc. The thesis: U.S. women's volleyball has the talent (NCAA dominance, Olympic gold medalists) but lacks integrated supply-chain infrastructure between youth and pro.

2020
ATHLETES COUNCIL

LOVB Athletes Council convenes.

Founded to support pro athletes into post-playing careers. All ten eventual Founding Athletes serve on the Council, anchoring athlete voice in league governance from inception.

2022

Series A — $16.75M.

First institutional financing. Junior club network expansion begins. The pipeline-building phase.

2022
CAPITAL

Series A · $16.75M.

First institutional financing round. Investors include Connect Ventures and several volleyball-aligned LPs. Cumulative capital: $16.75M.

2022
JUNIOR NETWORK

Junior club acquisition phase begins.

LOVB begins acquiring and affiliating with elite junior clubs across the U.S. — A5 Volleyball, Premier Nebraska, Houston Skyline among early integrations. The 90-club network builds from here.

2023

Series B + Founding Ten.

Six pro markets revealed. Founding Athlete equity model announced. Series B closes.

2023
CAPITAL

Series B · $35M.

Second institutional round. Investors include Atwater Capital (Vania Schlogel, lead). Cumulative capital: $51.75M. Validates pro-league go-forward.

2023
MARKETS

Six pro markets revealed.

LOVB announces the inaugural six pro markets: Atlanta · Austin · Houston · Madison · Omaha · Salt Lake. Markets chosen for U.S. volleyball heritage (Madison, Omaha, Salt Lake) and capital-market density (Austin, Houston, Atlanta).

2023
FOUNDING TEN

Founding Athletes announced.

Ten athletes take equity stakes alongside their playing commitments. Includes Jordan Larson, Lauren Carlini, Foluke Akinradewo Gunderson, Logan Eggleston, Kelsey Cook, Carli Lloyd, Jordan Thompson, Micha Hancock, Justine Wong-Orantes, Haleigh Washington. First athlete-equity model of its kind in U.S. women's pro sports at this scale.

2024

Series C — $100M.

Largest single-round investment in women's pro volleyball history. USA Network rights deal lands. Pro league finalizes.

2024 · Nov
CAPITAL

$100M Series C · Atwater leads.

Atwater Capital leads with Ares Management and Left Lane Capital participating. Cumulative capital: $151.75M+. The single largest women's pro volleyball round in U.S. history at that point.

2024 · Late
MEDIA

USA Network rights deal.

USA Network secures cable rights to LOVB Pro. Annual rights value ~$1M-$2M per CNBC reporting. First major U.S. women's volleyball cable rights deal.

2025

Inaugural season.

LOVB Pro Season One. Austin lifts the inaugural trophy. Ownership consolidation begins.

2025 · Jan
SEASON ONE

First Serve — inaugural season opens.

14-week regular season, six pro teams, USA Network primetime Wednesdays. The first U.S. women's pro volleyball league to ship at this scale.

2025 · Apr
CHAMPIONSHIP

LOVB Austin lifts inaugural trophy.

Austin defeats Atlanta 3-1 in the championship final. Logan Eggleston and Carli Lloyd anchor the title roster. Kelsey Cook (Atlanta) named inaugural Season MVP.

2025 · Jun
OWNERSHIP

LOVB Austin sold — Spurs + Bolt + G9.

First LOVB market sold. Spurs entities + Bolt Ventures + G9 Ventures as ownership group. Principals: Joshua Blitzer, Blake Griffin, Peter Holt.

2025 · Aug
OWNERSHIP

Nebraska rebrand · JE Dunn + Jordan Larson.

LOVB Omaha rebrands to LOVB Nebraska. JE Dunn Construction joins ownership alongside Founding Athlete Jordan Larson. "State of Legends" positioning.

2025 · Oct
EXPANSION

LOVB Los Angeles announced.

Alexis Ohanian's Seven Seven Six announced as LOVB LA ownership group. First confirmed 2027 expansion market.

2025 · Nov
OWNERSHIP

LOVB Houston · McNair family.

Lone Star Sports & Entertainment (McNair family / Houston Texans ownership) acquires LOVB Houston. NFL-scale operational expertise enters the market portfolio.

2026

Season Two · consolidation.

Houston dominates the regular season. Icons program launches. Salt Lake sold. Playoffs ship to Long Beach.

2026 · Jan 7
SEASON TWO

Season Two opens.

LOVB Nebraska at LOVB Austin opens Season Two. Same 14-week regular season cadence; USA Network primetime continues.

2026 · Feb 13-15
LOVB CLASSIC

LOVB Classic · Kansas City.

All six pro teams concentrated for the mid-season Classic event in Kansas City, MO. Anchors the league's mid-season storytelling.

2026 · Mar
OWNERSHIP

LOVB Salt Lake · Synergy.

Synergy Sports Capital (Terrence C. Murphy Sr. and Reggie Bush) acquires LOVB Salt Lake at reported $150M valuation. Fourth LOVB market sold.

2026 · Apr 8
ICONS LAUNCH

2026 LOVB Icons named.

First Team unanimous: Jordan Larson, Amber Igiede, Jordan Thompson. 14 athletes total across First and Second Team. Weighted 50% fan / 25% player / 25% coach voting.

2026 · Apr 10-12
PLAYOFFS · STAGE 1

Playoff semifinals · Louisville.

All semifinals concentrated at Norton Healthcare Sports & Learning Center. Non-LOVB-Pro market chosen for neutral ground and U.S. volleyball heritage.

2026 · Apr 16 + 18
PLAYOFFS · STAGE 2

Championship Weekend · Long Beach.

Final two matches at Long Beach Convention Center. Championship final airs primetime on USA Network. The first championship to ship in the post-Founding-Year era.

2027

Season Three · expansion.

Three new markets ship simultaneously — LA, Minnesota, San Francisco. Pro team count jumps from 6 to 9.

2027 · TBD
EXPANSION

LOVB Los Angeles · debut season.

Seven Seven Six (Alexis Ohanian) ownership. First confirmed of the three 2027 markets. Inaugural roster TBD.

2027 · TBD
EXPANSION

LOVB Minnesota · debut season.

Twin Cities market. Ownership group not yet publicly announced (as of May 2026). U.S. volleyball heritage market.

2027 · TBD
EXPANSION

LOVB San Francisco · debut season.

Bay Area market. Ownership group not yet publicly announced (as of May 2026). Highest-value capital market in the 2027 cohort.
