About · Who built this · And why

The platform behind the platform.

FieldCheck-LOVB is an independent capability layer for the LOVB ecosystem. Built and maintained from the ThinkDifferent Holdings portfolio. Our job is to make every player, coach, scout, club, parent more capable — by making the data layer they navigate clearer, more honest, and free of commercial incentive distortion.

§ I · The build

A capability platform, not a check.

FieldCheck started as something narrower — a "trust layer where retail loses to information asymmetry." A check before commitment. It still serves that purpose for other XCheck verticals like RentCheck and JobCheck. For LOVB, it became something else.

Volleyball doesn't have a trust crisis the way rentals or job offers do. What volleyball has is an information density problem. The pathway is opaque if you're new. The pro league is young. The athletes who anchor the league have stories most fans haven't yet read. The 90-club junior network is sprawling. The atlas didn't exist yet.

So we built it. Not as a check on LOVB — as a capability multiplier around it. The premise: everyone who interacts with the LOVB ecosystem becomes more capable when the data layer is clear, free, and source-grounded. Players make better pathway decisions. Coaches benchmark realistically. Scouts find prospects faster. Broadcasters write better pre-game packages. Parents ask sharper questions. Clubs see where they fit. LOVB itself becomes stronger by having an articulate independent ecosystem around it.

That's the build. Not an audit. Not a critique. A capability layer.

§ II · The operating principles

Seven commitments, in writing.

How a capability layer earns trust over time is by stating the rules and then living them. These are the seven operating principles. They appear on every persona-facing surface, and they govern every backlog decision.

1
Public-record sourcing.
Every claim cites a LOVB page, named publication, or public registry. The full source manifest lives at /lovb/sources.
2
CI on every score.
Where we offer probability bands (the Pathway tool), confidence intervals are always visible. Honest uncertainty over inflated confidence.
3
No school money.
No college, no club, no high school pays to be featured or ranked. We don't rank. We don't sell visibility.
4
No affiliate fees.
No commercial relationship with recruiting services, agents, coaching certifications, or any layer in the ecosystem we describe.
5
Walk-away default.
Every recommendation supports declining as a valid choice. We never frame the platform as a "must use" for anyone.
6
LOVB language stays LOVB's.
"Founding Athlete," "Whole Athlete Training," "Coach of Coaches," "Icons" — we cite, we don't claim. The language belongs to LOVB.
7
Athletes own their profiles.
Any athlete profiled here can claim and edit their own card details via the claim flow. Verification by LOVB-confirmed email or social handle.
§ III · The architecture

Where this lives.

FieldCheck-LOVB is one of several verticals under the XCheck portfolio, which sits inside ThinkDifferent Holdings. Other verticals include EdgeCheck (Internet-era commerce), FieldCheck-Sports (the umbrella platform with multi-sport architecture), RentCheck (rental vetting), SchoolCheck (K-12 evaluation), JobCheck (employer vetting). All share the "trust layer where retail loses to information asymmetry" thesis — but LOVB pivoted further toward the capability-layer framing because that's what the data ecosystem there actually needs.

The platform runs on a small static-site stack — Netlify for hosting, a Cloudflare Worker for the source-checker proxy. No tracking, no third-party analytics, no email-capture-and-resell pipelines. The Pathway tool runs entirely in the browser; nothing leaves your device unless you choose to submit.

The full source manifest, the change log, and the embed kit together describe everything we ship. There's no hidden tier, no "Pro," no premium content. Everything we publish, we publish freely.

§ IV · The relationship with LOVB

Independent. Aligned.

// What "independent" actually means

We don't take LOVB money.

FieldCheck-LOVB is not commercially affiliated with League One Volleyball Inc. We don't have a sponsorship, partnership, advertising relationship, or any financial flow from LOVB. We don't have a content licensing agreement. We don't have early access to LOVB news. We're cousins, not children.

What we are is aligned. We think LOVB's success matters because the broader U.S. women's volleyball ecosystem needs the league it built. If LOVB gets stronger because the data ecosystem around it gets stronger, that's a good outcome — for them, for us, for everyone in volleyball. That's the relationship. Independent in money, aligned in mission.

§ V · Contact + corrections

How to reach us.

If you're an athlete, coach, owner, parent, or scout mentioned anywhere on the platform and you want to claim, correct, or remove content — file a dispute. We honor takedown requests within 48 hours. Corrections inside 24.

If you're a journalist or researcher who'd like to source from the platform, everything we publish is CC BY-SA 4.0 licensed — share with attribution. No API key required for the embed kit; details at /lovb/embed.

If you're LOVB itself and want to suggest content updates, partnerships, or operational changes — the same dispute link reaches us. We treat LOVB-originated suggestions the same way we treat anyone else's: read fully, respond honestly, action what's warranted.
