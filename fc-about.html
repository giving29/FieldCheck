What FieldCheck
is.

Independent intelligence for the people who decide. Vetting before commitment. Tools, not pages.

The thesis

Trust layers where retail
loses to information asymmetry.

College and professional sports are full of high-stakes decisions made with bad information: where to play, who to recruit, which coach to trust, which path to take. The people closest to the money have the data. The people closest to the consequences mostly don't.

FieldCheck closes that gap. We take what's already public — schedules, rosters, statistics, transactions, contracts, recruiting databases, court records, news archives — and turn it into answers: projections, comparables, fit scores, roster grades. With confidence intervals. With citations. With the math visible.

The question isn't whether the data exists. It does. The question is whether anyone has bothered to make it usable for the athlete, the parent, the small program — not just the agency, the brand, or the front office. That's the gap.

How it works

Four principles.

01 · Public-record only

Every claim is sourced. If it isn't in a public database, official record, or established journalism, it isn't in FieldCheck. No insider whispers. No paywalled scrapes.

02 · Citation per claim

Show the work. Every projection links to the inputs that produced it. Every comparable shows the reasoning. Every roster grade exposes the math.

03 · Confidence over certainty

Ranges, not verdicts. Projections come with confidence intervals. Comparables come with similarity scores. Where we're guessing, we say so.

04 · Tools, not pages

Surfaces that answer one question. Not encyclopedias. Not endless feeds. Each tool exists to give you a specific answer to a specific question.

Sports we cover

Deepest in LOVB.
Expanding outward.

The deepest, most-developed surfaces in FieldCheck right now are the LOVB tools — pro projections, founding-athlete comparables, market fit, roster analysis. Women's volleyball is where the pattern is proven.

Football, basketball, and soccer are in beta — same methodology, fewer tools yet. The roadmap is to bring the LOVB tool pattern (project → match → fit → analyze) to every sport where the answer matters.

Independence

Built outside the incumbent stack.

FieldCheck is not owned by a media conglomerate, a recruiting service, an agency, a league, or a sports betting operator. It's an independent operation under ThinkDifferent Holdings.

That independence is the product. The day FieldCheck owes its data, its access, or its conclusions to anyone other than the user is the day it stops being useful.

What's next

Tools before features.

The next round of work is expanding the tool pattern to football, basketball, and soccer — same engine, sport-specific weights. Then deeper integration: prospect verdicts on the homepage, sport-specific dashboards, embed APIs for programs and journalists.

If you have a question we should be able to answer — and can't yet — tell us. The roadmap is responsive.
